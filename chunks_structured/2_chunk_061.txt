The ambitious four-movement Trio in D ma- jor is full of vitality and displays an extraordi- nary eagerness to experiment with regard to the specific conditions and potential of the genre. At the same time it is permeated by a kaleidoscopic variety of themes, which is typ- ical for this spirited woman composer with her broad education. She intentionally takes on these themes from different traditions and uses them to express many facets of her feelings and ability.
